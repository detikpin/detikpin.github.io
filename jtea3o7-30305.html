    Simon Pegg was born on February 14, 1970, in Gloucester, England. He developed an interest in comedy and acting at a young age and went on to study drama at the University of Bristol.     2. Breakthrough with “Spaced”:
    Pegg gained recognition for his work in the critically acclaimed sitcom “Spaced,” which he co-created and starred in alongside Jessica Hynes.     3. Collaboration with Edgar Wright:
    Simon Pegg formed a successful partnership with director Edgar Wright, resulting in the creation of the “Cornetto Trilogy.” This trilogy consists of the films “Shaun of the Dead” (2004), “Hot Fuzz” (2007), and “The World’s End” (2013).
